# AI Regulation and Policy

## Definition
The frameworks, laws, standards, and governance structures designed to manage AI development and deployment — balancing innovation enablement with safety, accountability, and public trust.

## Key Arguments
Three policy perspectives emerge:

1. **"Try-first" culture** ([[promoting-ai-adoption-medical-sector|US policy]], [[us-ai-strategy-try-first|US AI strategy]]): regulatory sandboxes and AI Centers of Excellence to enable controlled testing, supported by [[NIST]] and [[FDA]].

2. **Geopolitical sovereignty** ([[future-ai-healthcare-conflicts|Korean analysis]]): risk that global Big Tech captures national healthcare data and sells back refined products. AI sovereignty is a national security issue, not just an innovation question.

3. **Transparency requirements** ([[open-weights-vs-open-source-ai|OSI definition]]): the gap between "open weights" and truly "open source AI" has regulatory implications — incomplete transparency may not satisfy emerging compliance requirements.
