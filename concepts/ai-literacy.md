# AI Literacy

## Definition
The knowledge, skills, and critical thinking abilities needed to effectively use, evaluate, and make informed decisions about AI systems — encompassing technical understanding, practical proficiency, and ethical awareness.

## Key Arguments
Two perspectives illuminate what AI literacy requires:

1. **Cognitive awareness** ([[cognitive-debt-ai-brain|MIT study]]): AI literacy must include understanding [[Cognitive Debt]] — how AI use affects cognition. Sung's insight: "AI doesn't rot brains; we need to use AI better in teaching & learning." Literacy means knowing when to use AI and when to rely on unassisted thinking.

2. **Practical proficiency** ([[using-ai-right-now-quick-guide|Ethan Mollick]]): AI literacy is less about prompting techniques and more about knowing what features exist and using them on real work. Key skills: model selection, providing context, using Deep Research, multimodal capabilities, and understanding hallucination risks.
