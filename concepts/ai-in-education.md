# AI in Education

## Definition
The application of AI technologies to teaching, learning, assessment, and instructional design — encompassing AI as tutor, evaluator, course designer, and meta-cognitive tool.

## Key Arguments
Three distinct educational AI roles emerge from the sources:

1. **AI as meta-evaluator** ([[dean-of-llm-tutors|DeanLLM]]): LLMs can evaluate other LLMs' educational feedback at human-level accuracy, enabling quality assurance for AI-generated student feedback. Critical finding: smaller/cheaper tutor LLMs risk quality and safety.

2. **AI as course designer** ([[ai-individualized-learning-use-case|individualized learning]]): AI can generate complete competency-based course outlines from learner profile data, demonstrating practical AI-assisted instructional design.

3. **AI literacy as critical thinking** ([[iterative-prompt-design-ai-literacy|iterative prompt design]]): Sung's documented workflow shows that effective AI use requires clarifying intent before prompting, then iterating toward codified workflows. This reframes AI literacy as a critical thinking discipline.
