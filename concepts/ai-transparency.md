# AI Transparency

## Definition
The degree to which AI systems' internal processes, decision-making logic, training data, and development pipeline are accessible and understandable to external stakeholders — ranging from developers and regulators to end users.

## Key Arguments
Transparency operates at multiple levels:

1. **Process transparency** ([[monitoring-ai-chain-of-thought|DeepMind]]): CoT monitoring can reveal model reasoning, but only when tasks are complex enough to force genuine computation. The paradigm shift: design models that must reveal thinking, rather than trying to reverse-engineer opaque ones.

2. **Structural transparency** ([[persona-vectors-character-traits|Anthropic]]): persona vectors make internal model traits observable and controllable — moving transparency from output analysis to activation-space inspection.

3. **Pipeline transparency** ([[open-weights-vs-open-source-ai|OSI]]): Open Source AI requires full developmental pipeline access (code, data, checkpoints), not just final weights. This is essential for reproducibility, bias audits, and regulatory compliance.
