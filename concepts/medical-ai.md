# Medical AI

## Definition
The application of artificial intelligence — particularly large language models and multimodal systems — to clinical diagnosis, treatment planning, medical imaging, patient intake, and healthcare system management.

## Key Arguments
Medical AI has reached a critical inflection point. [[path-to-medical-superintelligence|MAI-DxO]] achieved 85% diagnostic accuracy on complex NEJM cases vs. ~20% for physicians, demonstrating AI's growing superiority in certain cognitive-diagnostic tasks. The [[physician-centered-oversight-diagnostic-ai|g-AMIE]] system shows AI can handle patient intake better than early-career doctors while maintaining physician oversight through asynchronous review.

However, significant barriers remain. The [[future-ai-healthcare-conflicts|Korean healthcare analysis]] identifies the "costly coexistence trap" — if trust and liability aren't resolved, AI adds cost rather than reducing it. [[promoting-ai-adoption-medical-sector|US policy documents]] emphasize that adoption speed, not technology, is the bottleneck. The [[us-ai-strategy-try-first|"try-first" approach]] proposes regulatory sandboxes to overcome institutional inertia.

A consensus is emerging: the question is not whether AI will transform medicine, but **how quickly** and **under whose control**. The geopolitical dimension (Korean specialized models vs. Big Tech foundation models) adds urgency.
