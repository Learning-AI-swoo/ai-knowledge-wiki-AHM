# AI Agents

## Definition
Autonomous or semi-autonomous software entities that use AI techniques to perceive, make decisions, take actions, and achieve goals — often coordinating multiple specialized agents to accomplish complex, multi-step tasks.

## Key Arguments
Two perspectives on AI agents emerge:

1. **Autonomous research agents** ([[autonomous-ai-research-agents|Stanford's Virtual Lab]]): Multi-agent teams can autonomously conduct scientific research with minimal human input (1.3% of total communication). The agents self-organize — selecting their own team composition, methodology, and tools. This compressed a weeks-long nanobody design process to days.

2. **Industry trajectory** ([[gartner-hype-cycle-2025|Gartner 2025]]): AI agents are at the Peak of Inflated Expectations but advancing rapidly. The shift is from GenAI as a novelty toward agents as operational infrastructure for sustainable AI delivery.

The key tension: agents are remarkably capable in structured domains but still require human oversight for safety-critical decisions (see [[Human-in-the-Loop Systems]]).
