# AI Champion Micro-Credential

## Overview
A structured credential program for AI literacy and practical AI skills, targeting faculty and health professionals at VUIM and beyond. The program aims to develop "AI Champions" — practitioners who can evaluate, adopt, and responsibly integrate AI into their professional workflows.

The credential operates in a market increasingly shaped by [[openai-certification-jobs-platform|OpenAI's 10M certification initiative]] (#034) and the [[us-presidential-ai-challenge|US Presidential AI Challenge]] (#035). Sung's differentiation: health-sciences focus, TCM context, critical-thinking-first pedagogy, and [[Cognitive Debt]] awareness built into the core curriculum.

## Assessment Framework (v0.1)

The [[ai-champion-competency-framework-v01|Competency Framework v0.1]] is the working assessment design. Key structural elements:

### Theoretical Foundation
- **3-tier progression**: AI-Curious → AI-Practitioner → AI-Champion, mapped to [[unesco-ai-competency-framework-teachers|UNESCO AI CFT]]'s Acquire/Deepen/Create levels
- **4D competency language**: Delegation, Description, Discernment, Diligence ([[Anthropic]])
- **Artifact + Commentary model**: adapted from edTPA; process = product
- **Pass/fail entrustment**: from CBME Entrustable Professional Activities
- **Observable behavior focus**: grounded in Long & Magerko (2020) and [[snail-ai-literacy-scale|SNAIL/TUCAPA]] model

### Tier Structure

| Tier | Core Question | Artifact | 4D Emphasis |
|---|---|---|---|
| AI-Curious | Can they decide when/where to use AI? | AI Opportunity Audit | Delegation + Diligence |
| AI-Practitioner | Can they produce real work with AI? | AI-Assisted Work Product + Prompt Log | Description + Discernment |
| AI-Champion | Can they teach others and redesign workflows? | Peer Teaching Portfolio + Proposal/Evaluation | All 4D + Teaching |

### Assessment Validation Strategy
- **Pre/post measurement**: [[snail-ai-literacy-scale|SNAIL questionnaire]] (31 items, TUCAPA model) as triangulation data
- **Pilot metrics** (V1): completion rate by tier, workflow adoption at 4 weeks, peer enablement
- **V2 roadmap**: scored rubric, CVI expert panel, inter-rater reliability, Open Badge Factory, Design-Based Research publication

## Curriculum Map

Eight modules derived from wiki analysis, each mapped to specific sources and concepts:

### Module 1: AI Landscape & Capabilities
**What it covers**: Current AI systems, capability levels, model selection
**Key sources**: [[oecd-ai-capability-indicators]] (#016), [[using-ai-right-now-quick-guide]] (#015), [[assessing-ai-job-interviews]] (#051)
**Key concept**: [[AI Literacy]]
**Outcome**: Participants can assess where AI currently stands (OECD L2-3), select appropriate models for their needs, and "interview" AI for their specific use cases

### Module 2: Critical Thinking → Prompt Engineering
**What it covers**: Intent clarification BEFORE prompting; iterative refinement; workflow codification
**Key sources**: [[iterative-prompt-design-ai-literacy]] (#024), [[understanding-in-context-learning]] (#001)
**Key concepts**: [[Prompt Engineering]], [[In-Context Learning]]
**Outcome**: Participants can design multi-stage AI workflows and codify them as reusable tools (e.g., CustomGPT)
**Pedagogical principle**: "AI literacy isn't about magic prompts — it's about clarifying your intent, iterating, and codifying workflows" (Sung, #024)

### Module 3: Context Engineering
**What it covers**: Token management, compaction, structured note-taking, sub-agent architectures
**Key sources**: [[context-engineering-ai-agents]] (#044), [[optimizing-tools-for-ai-agents]] (#038)
**Key concept**: [[Context Engineering]]
**Outcome**: Participants understand the "attention budget" concept and can design context-efficient AI interactions

### Module 4: Evaluating AI for Your Needs
**What it covers**: Beyond benchmarks — vibes testing, real-world evaluation, attitude profiling
**Key sources**: [[assessing-ai-job-interviews]] (#051), [[llm-benchmarks-survey]] (#031)
**Key concept**: [[LLM Benchmarks]]
**Outcome**: Participants can systematically evaluate AI models for their specific professional context
**Key quote**: "You wouldn't hire a VP based on SAT scores. Don't pick AI based on MMLU." (Mollick)

### Module 5: Cognitive Risks & Mitigation
**What it covers**: Cognitive debt, metacognitive laziness, time-saving illusion, when NOT to use AI
**Key sources**: [[cognitive-debt-ai-brain]] (#008), [[metacognitive-laziness-ai]] (#062), [[time-saving-illusion-ai]] (#064), [[navigating-ai-era-collaboration]] (#018)
**Key concept**: [[Cognitive Debt]]
**Outcome**: Participants can identify cognitive risk patterns in their own AI use and design mitigation strategies
**Sung's insight**: AI doesn't rot brains — but we need to use AI better in teaching and learning

### Module 6: AI Ethics & Integrity
**What it covers**: Academic integrity redefined; transparency frameworks; responsible AI use
**Key sources**: [[ai-rethinking-academic-integrity]] (#052), [[genai-transparency-framework-education]] (#075), [[debate-conscious-ai-model-welfare]] (#049)
**Key concepts**: [[AI in Education]], [[AI Transparency]]
**Outcome**: Participants adopt a transparency-first approach; can implement 4-level GenAI disclosure framework
**Key shift**: integrity = "owning your learning" (transparency + purpose), not "working alone" (isolation + prohibition)

### Module 7: Medical AI Applications
**What it covers**: Clinical AI tools, diagnostic AI, HITL design, hallucination awareness
**Key sources**: [[path-to-medical-superintelligence]] (#004), [[physician-centered-oversight-diagnostic-ai]] (#009), [[adversarial-hallucinations-medical-ai]] (#029), [[state-of-clinical-ai-2026]] (#060)
**Key concepts**: [[Medical AI]], [[Human-in-the-Loop Systems]], [[AI Hallucination]]
**Outcome**: Participants understand the "jagged frontier" of clinical AI and can evaluate medical AI tools

### Module 8: Equity & Access
**What it covers**: Digital divide, AI pricing tiers → inequality, teacher role protection
**Key sources**: [[ai-education-equity-crisis]] (#073), [[redefining-literacy-age-of-ai]] (#065), [[ai-future-education-unesco]] (#040)
**Key concepts**: [[AI in Education]], [[AI Regulation and Policy]]
**Outcome**: Participants understand equity implications of AI adoption and can advocate for inclusive implementation
**Core warning**: "The next digital divide is not about tech access but about the quality of human instruction surrounding it" (Kotran, #073)
